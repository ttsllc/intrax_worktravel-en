Introduction

We are excited to provide a valuable platform for individuals who have participated in Intrax/Ayusa programs to reconnect, explore further opportunities, and contribute to the diverse global experiences they have had. Why

Why not consider leveraging your international experience and work in Japan? Embarking on an overseas working experience can be a life-altering adventure, broadening your horizons and adding valuable weight to your resume. It serves as an investment in your career trajectory, empowering you to acquire crucial skills, and embrace cultural diversity while fostering a global vision, all of which will positively influence your future endeavors.

How

Find a potential job:
Use reputable resources from Intrax to explore work opportunities in Japan.

Apply for a job:
Tailor your application to each job you're interested in, highlighting your skills, experience, and passion for the role.

Obtain necessary documents:
Understand the visa requirements and any other legalities that may be necessary for your work abroad. Start the application process early to avoid any delays.

Prepare for the experience:
Educate yourself about the local culture, customs, and basic phrases in the local language. Being culturally aware will help you navigate and adapt to the new environment more smoothly.

Make arrangements for housing and transportation:
Secure accommodation close to your job site to minimize commuting hassles. Familiarize yourself with local transportation options for easy travel.

Pack for your trip:
Pack essential items and clothing appropriate for the climate and culture of the host country. Be mindful of any specific items you may need for your internship.

Enjoy the experience:
Embrace the opportunity to learn and grow both professionally and personally. Engage with colleagues and locals to build a network and immerse yourself in the local culture during your free time.

VISA

Visa requirements can differ based on your nationality, the host country, and the nature of the work. Understanding visas can be complex when pursuing an international job.

There are different categories of visas based on activity qualifications, such as “Technical and Humanities Knowledge” for which the visa type is categorized as an “Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services” visa. This category includes professionals in fields like mechanical engineering, interpretation, design, private enterprise, language instruction, and marketing. It’s important to note that management, administration, and accounting fall under a separate category of activity qualifications.
The duration of stay can vary from 5 years, 3 years, 1 year, 4 months (for management/administration only), to 3 months.



For American citizens, specific criteria are outlined:

A requirement of at least 3 years of practical experience in the related field of work is needed, supported by appropriate documentation. However, this is not necessary for individuals with a bachelor’s degree who are engaged in translation, interpretation, or language instruction.
A condition to receive compensation equal to or greater than that received by Japanese nationals engaging in the same work.



Working Holiday：

The Working Holiday program in Japan allows people from specific countries to stay in Japan for a designated period primarily for tourism and vacation purposes while also having the opportunity to work. Currently, 23 countries and regions are eligible for this program, and the number of participants has been increasing year by year, with around 15,000 foreigners visiting Japan on a Working Holiday visa.
A condition to receive compensation equal to or greater than that received by Japanese nationals engaging in the same work.

View of the Working Holiday Program

  • Mainly targeted at individuals aged 18 to 30 (35 for Australia and Canada).
  • Allows staying in Japan for a specified period, such as one year, primarily for vacation purposes.
  • Accompanying children or spouses is prohibited.
  • It’s possible to work to support one’s stay during the period.
  • Applicants must not have been previously issued a Working Holiday visa.

Countries Eligible for the Working Holiday Program:

  • North America and South America: Canada, Argentina, Chile.
  • Europe: France, Germany, theUnited Kingdom, Ireland, Denmark, Norway, Portugal, Poland, Slovakia, Austria, Hungary, Spain, Iceland, Czech Republic, Lithuania, Sweden, Estonia, the Netherlands.
  • Oceania: Australia, New Zealand.
  • Asia: South Korea, Taiwan, HongKong.
    • Note: Acceptance quotas, conditions, and stay durations vary by country.
